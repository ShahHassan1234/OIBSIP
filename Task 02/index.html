Biography

Cristiano Ronaldo, born on February 5, 1985, in Funchal, Madeira, Portugal, is a legendary professional soccer player widely regarded as one of the greatest in the sport's history. He began his career at Sporting Lisbon's youth academy, later joining Manchester United in 2003 and winning three English Premier League titles and a UEFA Champions League title. In 2009, he moved to Real Madrid, where he became the club's all-time leading scorer and won four Champions League trophies. Ronaldo then played for Juventus in Italy before returning to Manchester United in 2021. Internationally, he led Portugal to victory in the 2016 UEFA European Championship and the 2019 UEFA Nations League. Renowned for his goal-scoring prowess and versatility, Ronaldo has won multiple FIFA Ballon d'Or awards. Off the field, he is involved in philanthropy and various business ventures, cementing his status as a global icon.
